Palak Paneer with Jeera Rice

⚠ Age Note (youngest eater: 3 years) Paneer should be cut into small, soft cubes (1 cm). Reduce whole spices in tempering. Ensure rice is fully cooked and slightly soft. No whole peppercorns in the child's portion.
Ingredients
  • Fresh spinach (palak)4 cups
  • Paneer, cubed250 g
  • Onion, finely chopped1 large
  • Garlic cloves, minced4 cloves
  • Ginger, grated1 tsp
  • Tomatoes, chopped2 medium
  • Cumin seeds (jeera)1 tsp
  • Garam masala½ tsp
  • Heavy cream3 tbsp
  • Basmati rice1½ cups
  • Oil2 tbsp
  • Saltto taste
Instructions
  1. 1
    Blanch spinach in boiling water for 2 minutes, then transfer to ice water. Drain and blend into a smooth purée. Set aside.
  2. 2
    Heat oil in a pan over medium heat. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter. Add onions and sauté until golden, about 7 minutes.
  3. 3
    Add garlic and ginger. Sauté for 1 minute until fragrant. Add tomatoes and cook until soft and oil separates, about 5 minutes.
  4. 4
    Add the spinach purée and mix well. Season with garam masala and salt. Simmer for 5 minutes.
  5. 5
    Add paneer cubes and cream. Stir gently and simmer for 3–4 minutes. Do not over-stir or paneer will break.
  6. 6
    For jeera rice: rinse basmati, cook with 1:1.75 water ratio and ½ tsp cumin seeds until fluffy. Fluff with a fork.
  7. 7
    Serve palak paneer over jeera rice. Garnish with a swirl of cream and a pinch of garam masala.
